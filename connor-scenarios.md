# Connor McBride — Scenario Q&A and Work Experience

## Software Demos
Connor has over 5 years of experience running software demos, primarily focused on selling add-ons, product upgrades, and location-based expansions to existing customers like senior living operators and elective medicine practices. Before each demo, he refreshes himself on the customer's goals and pain points and tailors the flow accordingly rather than running the same script every time. He walks customers through the solution from both internal and external user perspectives, connecting outcomes directly to their goals and bottom-line value. He also guided existing customers through customizing virtual assistant workflows based on what had proven successful with similar clients. When running complex demos, he leads with business impact, user experience, and ROI rather than tactical details, and stays composed when something unexpected comes up.

## Account Expansion Approach
Before introducing an expansion or add-on conversation, Connor makes sure he is deeply knowledgeable about the customer's business, community portfolio, and where the service is already being used. Monthly business reviews are where he uncovers unmet needs, and that information shapes where he sees growth opportunities. Expansion was typically the lowest hanging fruit because he could point directly to results already being driven at other locations. The ROI case was compelling on its own — a single conversion could outweigh the monthly cost by a ratio of 50:1. He focuses on making customers feel like they're building something together rather than being sold to.

### HumanGood Expansion Case Study (250% NRR)
HumanGood started on a pilot program serving 10 of their 25 locations at $500/month per location. Connor's goal was to onboard the initial 10 and expand to the rest within 6 months. Onboarding and time to value took about 60 days. He discovered their main KPI was move-ins and website conversion, so he set up a CRM integration and automated monthly move-in by lead source reports. He appointed a power user at each location as his point of contact for adoption signals. He also noticed "tour" was showing up as a hot keyword in chat transcripts, pulled examples, and used this as an upsell opportunity. After 5 months, each pilot location had seen move-ins and enhanced customer service. Between the lead source reports and positive feedback from power users, they expanded to the rest of their portfolio. After the first year, this account ended with 250% NRR.

## Readiness Indicators for Upgrades
The first 90 days of service are the most critical for indicating expansion potential. Post-onboarding, Connor assigns a power user at each location (usually the sales director) and maintains an open email thread to stay on top of adoption rates, bugs, and best practices. He encourages sharing wins and pain points across locations. Once he has enough data from day-to-day users, he brings findings and the use case to the executive sponsor during the next monthly review. Low adoption or unresolved issues are a clear sign to hold off on upgrade conversations until the foundation is solid.

## Re-engaging Stagnant Accounts
When a satisfied but disengaged client has been in maintenance mode, Connor takes a targeted approach rather than a routine check-in. He pulls together their usage data and results as a baseline, then reaches out with a specific agenda tied to their goals and new product developments. On the call, he asks open-ended questions to uncover unmet business goals and positions features as complementary to achieving them. He focuses on solving problems rather than just selling. He leaves every meeting with clear action items and builds relationships across the org at different roles and levels.

## Portfolio Management (70+ Accounts)
Connor segments accounts based on customer journey stage, ARR, expansion potential, and health score. High-potential accounts (high usage, getting value but not using all features or not deployed across the full org) get monthly meetings with a multithreaded approach building product champions and executive sponsorship. Lower-value accounts get a tech-touch approach supported by self-serve resources like knowledge bases and webinars, with automated email sequences and alerts for usage changes. Time split: roughly 60% high-value accounts, 20% systems and monitoring for smaller accounts, 20% internal work. At SiteStaff he used HubSpot for tracking, reminders, meetings, contacts, and NRR. At Ogury he used Salesforce for campaign setup, optimizations, collaboration logging, and billing.

## Handling Escalations
When handling escalations like slow ticket resolution, Connor first reviews the client's recent ticket history to identify where breakdowns are happening and whether it's a pattern. He brings context to internal teams and asks them to prioritize. He then goes back to the customer with a concrete follow-up point without overpromising. He sets up notifications to monitor progress and once stabilized, walks the client through internal SLAs. On the internal side, he works with support to identify root causes and prevent recurrence.

## Saving At-Risk Accounts
At SiteStaff, Connor had an account where engagement and usage dropped 60 days into service despite strong chat volumes and leads. He reached out to power users at each location and quickly found they weren't seeing chat transcripts in their CRM due to a field mapping issue. He identified the problem by comparing field names between systems, brought in the product team and the CRM's technical solutions team, and resolved it same-day. He set up automated monthly lead source reports to prevent recurrence. The account was saved and renewed the next year.

When a key stakeholder explores competitors, Connor stays calm and doesn't get defensive. He thanks the stakeholder for being upfront, asks open-ended questions to understand what's driving the decision, and suggests a follow-up meeting. He escalates internally, reaches out to other contacts in the account, digs into usage data, and builds a presentation grounded in real numbers showcasing existing value, switching risks, and addressing concerns directly. After saving an account, he focuses on building broader relationships to hear about problems early.

## Onboarding Approach
Connor's onboarding is milestone-based. At SiteStaff, first value was separated into three categories: leads, enhanced customer service, and move-ins. After a deal is signed, he sets up a discovery session with stakeholders from each location to outline the process, what to expect during the first 30/60/90 days, and questions about their community's culture of care. The flow: discovery call > webinar > knowledge base setup > CRM integration > go live. He records a walkthrough webinar, sends a go-live email to executive stakeholders and power users, and sets biweekly business reviews for the first 60 days. For larger clients he breaks onboarding into groups of five locations at a time. During early reviews he walks through transcripts showing progress toward the three milestones.

## CS-Sales Collaboration on Renewals
CS owns the day-to-day relationship; Sales handles contract negotiation. About 60 days before renewal, Connor connects with the sales rep to review the year's performance and do a risk assessment covering renewal probability and any ACV changes. During customer calls, Connor owns the story around performance, value delivery, and goals, while Sales owns the negotiation and contract. Expansion was fully owned by CS.

## Voice of the Customer
Connor keeps records of product and reporting enhancement requests. Once he has a clear story with themes, he presents findings during product calls. A strong example: at SiteStaff, the core product was managed live chat. Around mid-2023, competitors started targeting their key verticals with virtual sales assistants (automated decision trees converting traffic into leads). As the sole CSM closest to these signals, he gathered client feedback, demoed a competitor tool for the product team, and made the case to build their own version with a key differentiator: integrating live chat into the VSA workflow so clients could get automation volume with human conversation quality. The product team aligned on that vision and prioritized it on the roadmap.
